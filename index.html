What is Stableford?

Stableford is a points-based format. Instead of counting total strokes, you score points on every hole based on how your net score compares to par — highest total points wins, not lowest strokes. A blow-up hole only costs you that hole's points (minimum 0), so one bad hole can't wreck your round.

Format for this trip

Individual Net Stableford. Each player competes on their own — handicap strokes are applied per player so everyone has a fair chance regardless of ability.

Handicap Strokes — how many shots you get, and where

  1. Each player has a Handicap Index (see the Players tab) — fixed for the trip.
  2. Each course converts that Index into a Course Handicap using its own Slope Rating and Course Rating. This page does that math for you automatically.
  3. Everyone plays their own full Course Handicap against scratch (0) — there's no adjustment relative to the group.
  4. Course Handicap strokes are given on the hardest holes first, using each hole's Stroke Index (SI 1 = hardest, SI 18 = easiest). A Course Handicap over 18 gets a 2nd stroke on the hardest holes.
  5. Net score on a hole = Gross strokes − handicap strokes received on that hole.

Winning the trip

Each round is scored separately; total Stableford points across all 4 rounds are added up on the Leaderboard tab. Most total points after 4 rounds wins. Round-by-round results are worth calling out too — daily bragging rights / side bets.

Quick reference

  • Play the ball as it lies — honor-system trip, not a Rules of Golf exam. Use common sense for lost balls / unplayable lies (count it a net double bogey and move on).
  • If a hole is clearly going to score 0 points, pick up and move to the next tee — normal Stableford practice, keeps play moving.
  • Ties on the Leaderboard: countback on the last round played, then the last 9 holes of that round.
